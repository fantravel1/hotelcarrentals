Questions Answered

Everything You Need to Know About Hotel + Car Travel

Real answers to the questions travelers actually ask.

Do I need a rental car in [CITY]?

It depends on your itinerary. Cities like New York and San Francisco are better without a car in the city center. But you might need one for day trips to wine country, national parks, or coastal drives.

Our recommendation: Use our City Intelligence tool to see if your planned activities require a car. Most travelers need a car for 2-3 days max.

Check your destination ‚Üí

Should I rent a car at the airport or downtown?

Airport rentals charge facility fees, concession fees, and local taxes that can add 25-40% to your total. Downtown locations are often cheaper and more convenient if you don't need a car immediately.

Best strategy: Take a taxi/Uber to your hotel, explore car-free for a few days, then pick up a rental downtown for your road trip days.

See airport fee breakdown ‚Üí

What hotels make car rental easy?

Look for hotels with free parking (or reasonable rates), proximity to rental locations, easy highway access, and honest parking policies. Hotels near downtown rental offices are ideal for "car days" strategy.

HotelCarRentals Verified: We badge hotels that excel at car rental convenience, transparent parking costs, and flexible access.

Browse verified hotels ‚Üí

How much does hotel parking cost?

It varies wildly. Budget hotels: $10-20/day. Downtown luxury hotels: $40-75/day (or more). Valet-only can add $60+/day. Some hotels charge resort parking fees on top of nightly rates.

Hidden costs: In/out privileges (extra fees each time you leave), mandatory valet, overnight "storage" fees, and EV charging markups.

See parking costs by city ‚Üí

How do I plan a trip with car days and no-car days?

Start with your itinerary. Mark days you'll be exploring the city (no car needed). Then identify days for road trips, national parks, or areas with poor transit. Rent only for those specific days.

Example: Barcelona 5-day trip ‚Üí Days 1-3 metro/walk in the city, Days 4-5 rent a car for Costa Brava and return it before flying home.

Use our Car Days Planner ‚Üí

What is the real cost of renting a car?

The advertised rate is just the start. Add: insurance (if not covered by credit card), fuel (often with ripoff pre-pay policies), tolls, parking, airport/local taxes, and potential damage fees.

Real example: $30/day advertised ‚Üí actual cost $65-85/day after all fees in high-tax markets like Las Vegas or Miami.

Rental Truth Library

No-BS Guides to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

Everything rental companies don't want you to know

01

What to Photograph Before Leaving the Lot

Document every scratch, dent, tire condition, fuel level, and mileage. Take photos of the rental agreement terms. This prevents 90% of damage disputes.

Essential
02

The Real Cost of Rental Car Insurance

Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) costs $15-35/day. Your credit card likely covers this. Personal Auto Insurance may extend to rentals. Don't double-pay.

Money Saver
03

Fuel Policies Explained: Full-to-Full vs Prepay

Full-to-full: return with same fuel level (best option). Prepay: you pay for a full tank upfront at inflated prices. Never choose "we'll fill it for you."

Avoid Scams
04

How Toll Roads Work by Country

USA: E-ZPass/SunPass systems. Europe: Vignettes, autopay, or toll booths. Rental companies charge admin fees for tolls. Buy your own toll pass when possible.

Country Guide
05

Airport Rental Fees Breakdown

Customer Facility Charges ($8-15/day), Concession Recovery Fees (10-15%), local/state taxes (8-20%), stadium taxes, tourism taxes. Total: 25-40% extra.

Fee Truth
06

What Happens After a Rental Car Accident

Stop immediately. Call police (required in most countries). Document everything. Call rental company. Don't admit fault. Check your coverage before signing damage waivers.

Emergency Guide
07

Common Rental Car Scams to Avoid

Forced upgrades, fake damage claims, mandatory insurance lies, prepaid fuel scams, cleaning fee traps, and "you scratched the windshield" cons.

Scam Alert
08

International Driver's Permit: When You Actually Need One

Required in Japan, required by some EU rental companies, not required in UK/Ireland/Canada/Australia. Check your destination's rules ‚Äî rental companies often lie about this.

Legal Requirement
